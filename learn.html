Learn the method to our madness

October 12, 2022
By Leonardo Masini

This trick may seem like it is exclusive for the human calculators that you see on tv shows. But this is something anybody can do, after putting in the practice you will be able to know the correct day of the week for any given date.

This is just a quick tutorial that I decided to write, I recommend that you check out Numberphile's video on this called "The Doomsday Algorithm". And if for some reason you still want to read my tutorial, here it is:

Doomsday dates

No, by Doomsday we don't mean the end of the world or D-day from WWII. The doomsday dates are certain dates that always fall on the same day of the year; 4/4/2022 is on the same weekday as 12/12/2022, or 9/5/2022 because these are all doomsday dates. In the year this is being written(2022), the doomsday dates all fall on monday, and the day of the week for all of these changes every year. Next year it will be tuesdays

That is a cool little fact, but what's important is that you memorize the doomsday dates.

Even months

The even numbers are the easiest because they have an obvious pattern; the month and day are the same numbers

4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12 are all doomsday dates

Note that 2/2 is not in the list, this has to do with the leap year, we'll talk about how this impacts January and February later on

Odd months

Sadly these are trickier, for these you have to memorize pairs of two numbers that are linked together. For instance 5 and 9 are linked together; 5/9 and 9/5 are both doomsdates.

The two pairs are 5/9 - 9/5, 7/11 - 11/7. And 7/11 is an easy pair to remember because of the corner store

January and February

These are affected by the leap year therefore their respective doomsdates change depending on the year. On february, the doomsdate is always the last day of the year -- 2/28 on normal years and 2/29 on the leap year. And because weeks go in multiples of 7, this means that the doomsdate for a normal year in february, 2/28, extends to any other multiple of 7(2/7, 2/14, 2/21 are all doomsdates). This is not necessary but can be helpful later on

March has one on pi day(3/14) and thus any day that is a multiple of 7. Januaries have a doomsdate on 1/3 normally, and in a leap years it is 1/4. These are the trickiest and the ones I recommend learning last.

Finding the day of the week

Once you have an understanding of the doomsdates you are almost ready to guess the day for any year you know the doomsday day. On 2022, the doomsday day is monday, therefore if you are given calendar date-- 10/13/2022 -- you can use the doomsday date of that month which is 10/10, a monday

[13 - 10 = 3] 3 days after monday is thursday. At first these take some time, but the more you do, the faster you get

A tougher example would be finding the day for something like 9/29. You can use the standard doomsdate for that month(9/5) and just like before find the difference between both days [29-5 = 24], 24 days after monday is not very easy to do in your head, so we can remove the extra cycles using a math concept called modulus

You can do 24 mod 7, which means divide 24/7 and get the remainder; 24/7 = 3 with a remainder of 3*, you can take that 3* and add it to doomsday day of the week for 2022; monday

3 days after monday: Thursday

2/5 is an interesting one because the given doomsday date is bigger: 2/28. Personally, I would take that 28 and, like mentioned earlier, modify it with the knowledge that it is a multiple of 7. You can make 2/7 your doomsday date and go two days back(2/7 -> 2/5), making it a saturday. Or if you don't like going back in days, you can use the doomsdate 2/0. Day 0 obviously doesn't exist but can be useful when guessing days of the week: [Day 5 - Day 0 = 5] 5 days after monday: Saturday
